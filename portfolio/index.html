NeurIPS 2025 Workshop · Symmetry and Geometry in Neural Representations

Saliency Thresholds in Neural Code and its Relation to the Power-Law, Gaussian, and Lambert W Function

Co-authored with Jin Hyun Park and Yoonsuck Choe

Accepted to the NeurIPS 2025 Workshop on Symmetry and Geometry in Neural Representations (Extended Abstract Track). This work explores a theoretical juncture where the power law, Gaussian distribution, and Lambert W function converge to relate to saliency thresholds in neural code.

The cortical neurons' response properties are peculiar: despite variability in stimulus distribution, the response has a stereotypic heavy-tail distribution. We show that when this power-law-like response distribution is compared with a matched Gaussian distribution, the intersection point is linearly correlated with the saliency threshold. This intersection can be analytically computed using the Lambert W function and is also linearly correlated with the standard deviation of the response.

The Lambert W function emerges naturally in instances where exponential processes (like the Gaussian's exponentially decaying tails) and polynomial processes (like the power law's polynomially decaying tails) meet—appearing across diverse phenomena from brain oxygen coupling to enzyme kinetics and evolutionary models.

These results point to an invariant property in neural thresholding that could greatly simplify downstream processing in biological visual systems and potentially extends across different sensory modalities and processing levels.

BeatBreeder

Evolving percussion sounds through interactive selection using CPPN-NEAT (Compositional Pattern Producing Networks with NeuroEvolution of Augmenting Topologies).

BeatBreeder is an open-ended creative tool that applies evolutionary computation to sound design. Users can evolve unique percussion sounds by selecting their favorites from each generation, allowing the system to breed increasingly complex and interesting sonic textures.

How It Works

The system uses CPPNs (Compositional Pattern Producing Networks) to generate audio waveforms. Each network takes time as input and outputs audio samples. Through NEAT (NeuroEvolution of Augmenting Topologies), the network topology evolves over generations, starting simple and growing more complex based on user selections.

This approach mirrors biological evolution: mutations introduce variations, crossover combines successful patterns, and human aesthetic judgment acts as the selection pressure. The result is an exploration of sound space that would be impossible to navigate through traditional parameter-tweaking interfaces.

Volk

Building, scaling, and exiting e-commerce brands through organic social media marketing during the early era of TikTok and Instagram's creator economy.

The Journey

From December 2019 to December 2023, my friends and I built multiple e-commerce brands that collectively achieved over 100 million impressions and millions of organic followers across social platforms. We pioneered what would later become formalized as "organic social media marketing"—a strategy that has since become standard practice in digital commerce.

What We Built

We didn't just sell products—we built brands with distinct personalities and loyal communities. Our approach was unconventional for the time: instead of paid advertising, we focused on creating entertaining, shareable content that happened to feature our products. We treated social media accounts as entertainment channels first and storefronts second.

This strategy paid off. Multiple six and seven-figure exits validated our approach, and the methodology we developed through trial and error in the "wild west era" of social commerce has since become a standard playbook in the industry.

Lessons Learned

Beyond the financial success, Volk taught me about rapid iteration, understanding audience psychology, and building systems that scale. It was a masterclass in pattern recognition—identifying what content would resonate before it went viral, understanding platform algorithms through empirical testing, and adapting quickly to changing dynamics.

While I'm no longer actively involved in e-commerce, the experience of building something from zero to millions of impressions, managing teams, and navigating high-stakes exits has been invaluable. It taught me that interesting problems exist everywhere, and that the principles of experimentation, measurement, and iteration apply just as well to building businesses as they do to research.

Molly & The Mop

Connecting commercial and residential service providers with clients through a social-first marketplace platform.

The Vision

Molly & The Mop began with a simple observation: local service providers—cleaners, landscapers, handymen—often lacked an effective way to reach customers beyond word-of-mouth, while customers struggled to find reliable, vetted service providers in their area.

We built an online platform that solved both sides of this problem. Service providers could create profiles, showcase their work, and receive bookings directly through the platform. Customers could browse verified providers, read reviews, and schedule services with confidence.

Growth & Community

The company grew to 15+ employees and built a social media following of over 85,000 people. We leveraged the same organic content strategy from Volk, but adapted it for the service industry—sharing satisfying before/after transformations, highlighting local providers, and building a community around home improvement and maintenance.

What started as a simple marketplace evolved into a trusted brand in the Houston area. Our social presence drove consistent organic traffic to the platform, and our focus on vetting quality providers built trust on both sides of the marketplace.

The Exit

In December 2023, we sold Molly & The Mop to a private local cleaning company. They saw value not just in the technology platform, but in the community and brand we'd built. The customer base, provider network, and social media presence provided them with immediate market presence and credibility.

This venture reinforced lessons about marketplace dynamics, two-sided network effects, and the importance of brand in commodity service industries. It was rewarding to build something that genuinely helped local businesses grow while making it easier for people to maintain their homes and workspaces.
