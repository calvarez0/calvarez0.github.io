Research

Neural Vector Calculations

In a bee's central cortex, there are sets of columnar (vertical) and tangential (horizontal) neurons that perform vector calculations. These calculations are responsible for the bee’s allocentric orientation of the world (understanding north and south as opposed to just forwards and backwards) and for navigating to resources (pollen is 2 km away, 20 degrees to the right of the sun). We are using neuroevolutionary algorithms to understand how these neural vector calculations might emerge from simple inputs and environments.
