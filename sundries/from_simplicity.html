From Simplicity, Complexity

Boids: Starling Murmuration Simulation

Three simple rules give rise to complex, lifelike flocking behavior:

  • Separation: Avoid crowding neighbors
  • Alignment: Match velocity with neighbors
  • Cohesion: Move toward the center of nearby flockmates

Braitenberg Vehicles: Synthetic Psychology

These simple "vehicles" demonstrate how basic sensor-motor connections can create behaviors that appear surprisingly intelligent:

  • Direct connections between light sensors and wheels (green vehicles)
  • Crossed connections between sensors and wheels (red vehicles)
  • Press 'T' to toggle trajectory visualization
  • Press 'L' to toggle vehicle lights
  • Use arrow keys to move the light sources

Valentino Braitenberg showed how the simplest of mechanisms can create behavior that we might describe as fear, aggression, love, or exploration - a powerful lesson in the relationship between mechanism and meaning.

The Power of Simple Rules

These simulations demonstrate a profound truth about complex systems: sophisticated, lifelike behavior doesn't require sophisticated rules. From the murmurations of starlings to the patterns of human crowds, complexity often emerges from the interaction of simple components following simple rules.
